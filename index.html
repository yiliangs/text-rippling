text-rippling

Move your mouse.

A tiny zero-dependency library for mouse-reactive per-character text effects on the web.

repel

characters scatter away from the cursor with a soft spring return

attract

the opposite — letters lean toward your pointer like iron filings

jelly

soft, scaling, slightly tilted — push them around

magnify

chars under the cursor swell up, distance only scales

wave

a horizontal sine wave, modulated by cursor proximity

scatter

each letter has a private direction; cursor controls intensity

swirl

tangential push — letters orbit around the cursor

wake

chars scramble and glow in the cursor's wake, fading slowly behind

glow

a soft luminous trail follows your cursor — wake without the scramble

redact — stochastic block redaction with morphing turnover

Hold your cursor anywhere over this paragraph. Inside the inner radius every character collapses into a solid block; in the outer fringe band the redaction thins out with distance, and twice each second roughly thirty percent of the redacted glyphs slide back to their original form while the same number of unredacted glyphs flip into blocks alongside them, so the total density holds steady but the membership keeps churning. Drag the cursor and the redaction zone follows you, eating new text at the leading edge and exhaling old text behind. Stand still and the morph still runs underneath, an unsettled black bar that never quite settles on which glyphs it has chosen to hide.
